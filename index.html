Old Memories

I think it was really early on, probably like 10.
I used to play with my parents' phones sometimes and try to see if I could go on the internet,
but at the time you couldn't.
My parents had a really, really old computer or laptop,
and that's when I started finding YouTube and understanding what YouTube was and finding music in there
and just engaging also with TV, with music.
My mom used to have a music channel that she would play.
Finding sources of music and what was new, what was English music.
At the time I would mainly listen to Spanish,
but then also starting to learn and see what English music was about.
I would go to my cousin's house. I didn't have a gaming drive like other people.
So I just paid attention to understand what Minecraft was, what was Roblox,
and also playing dress-up games online.

...

Lady Gaga was big. The Black Eyed Peas were really big, Pitbull was really big.
Having access to Qubo, stuff like that too, didn't have cable.
I was probably late years of elementary, like fifth grade, fourth grade.
It would mainly be Qubo and stuff that was free or channel 8 with PBS.
That was my medium, like Elmo and stuff like that.
I think we still watch it to this day.

...

...fondest memories of Minecraft?

The aspect of building something. You can create your own world,
but also get frustrated when something goes wrong.
I would see my cousins crash out when something went wrong.
It's kind of intense but also relaxing at the same time.
You get to hear all the music in the background that Minecraft had,
or the little sounds that the little person makes.
It was just you and your world basically.

...

...games on Roblox?

I used to play scary games sometimes.
The natural disaster ones where you have to find shelter.
We would all play as a trio and support each other as they played.

...

...any particular TV shows?

I would watch WordGirl on PBS.
I really liked her because she was brown and I'm brown.
I could relate to her because she had a double life.
She had to manage her family stuff but also manage the world.
Save the world but also be home.

...

I thought the world was actually bigger, like it would be in El Salvador,
but also being in the US.
Wanting to be in both places expanded my view of the world.
I wasn't just in Texas, which helped me not see my world as narrow.
I think being downtown or getting in the plane, like of course getting in the plane when you're young,
you're like, oh my God, you're so tiny compared to everything else.
I think also getting lost in stores as a kid, that feels like endless.
You can be in the store or the library for hours.

...

...main way of accessing the internet?

I had a laptop too, but it started out with my cousins.
Getting access to the internet was a little hard at the time.
As I started getting older it was an investment.
As you grow older, you start seeing less of your cousins.
Getting your own computer and sometimes having sleepovers with them.

...

...any web series or music videos?

I would watch lifestyle videos, like Seven Super Girls and
they would talk about how their room was decorated and how they had access,
lived in a big neighborhood and they were white, of course.
My cousin was telling me how they decorated the room and how she wanted it like that.
Also watching novelas at a really young age with my family
even though I wasn't allowed to watch the kissing scenes.
The YouTube videos of lifestyle, beauty, life hacks,
or what high school is supposed to look like, that peaked back then.
I remember before I went to high school I'd watch those "how to survive high school" videos.
I literally bought a book like "how to survive college."

...

...any YouTubers you remember?

David Dobrik. Wasabi Productions, they were so funny.
And lifestyle and beauty channels basically.
There were YouTubers who would do "what's in my emergency kit"
or "get ready with me to school" morning routines.
I feel like YouTube introduced me to beauty.
I mainly got into it through YouTube, not really my mom.

...

...was it escapism or something to look forward to?

Something to look forward to later on.
But also a way to create art in different forms.
I feel like I stopped doing art because it was never a priority I guess of mine.
So then makeup was a different way of art to me. Or it still is.
You got to perfect it and understand that one little thing can set it off.
It was about forming your identity, finding out what fits you,
finding your own groove. Making it work with what you have.
I didn't have enough time to watercolor or didn't have the supplies.
Makeup was different because makeup was more practical back then.
I didn't live by an area where we had Michaels. We just had Walmart.
I would save money and what I'd see in the grocery store is makeup,
seeing all the lines of makeup and just trying to be like the YouTube video.
A lifestyle basically. It was accessible in a way.

...first websites you ever interacted with?

Poptropica, remember that stupid website. They shut it down.
GirlsGoGames. Roblox.
...
It was a heavy laptop, like two times heavier.
It was a VAIO or something like that. We still have it at my house.

I love to play Cool Math Games.
I still play that running game to this day. Run.
There was also ABC Mouse.
It started coming out as we were about to leave elementary.

...how did you learn to navigate a computer?

My cousin was a geek with computers.
He made my email account when I was really young.
I didn't pay attention until I realized it's an actual email you can use.
Learning what Netflix and Spotify and Pandora was.
They were hella advanced with media. They knew about the gamer channels.

...

Movie Star Planet, you get to dress your Hollywood star and then you would chat.
Just literally talking to randoms. I think I was trying to actually be friendly.

...

...any games you still go back to?

Cool Math Games and Roblox to this day.
I played the granny game on Roblox. Dress to Impress.
I really liked the Roblox game where you got to build a whole life,
you were able to build your own lifestyle and change cars and drive.

...

...did they feel like safe spaces?

I think Roblox did. You could always do what you wanted. They were all chill.

...

...how did they shape your identity?

They gave me some hope. I still think about that little Jeep I used to drive,
those cars and what they looked like back then. The graphics of the game.
My cousins would talk about GTA, FIFA, Call of Duty.
They had an Xbox, a Wii, Nintendos.
When they released those cute glittery DS collections,
I was like I need to have one just for the aesthetic.
I had the pink one and I still have it.
I also had a PS Vita. I had a Shrek game on there.
I had the Mario Kart game too.
I also had movies on the PS. Karate Kid came with it. I love that movie.
I remember when he kissed the girl. Me and my grandma were like... right.

...

...any TV shows?

More like Barbie movies, that was my jam.
The Shrek ones of course. Open Season was hilarious.

...Barbie movies that stuck with you?

The Princess and the Pauper. Me and my cousin's favorite movie.
You're just like me. Yes that one.
Barbie and the Mermaid Tale, the Fashion Fairy Tale.
All of them are my jam.
In my apartments, the guy would come and sell the movies and my dad would get them.
Or my cousin had them. Either one of us.

The second one. I watched it like 300 times.
Nobody can tell me nothing about Shrek.

Dora too. I was in love with Dora. And Diego.
With Dora she just always had the freedom to go anywhere.
She didn't care, just her with that little backpack and monkey.

Barbie in the Dream House. She just does her own thing.

She's just that girl.

El Chavo. Atrevete a Soñar. Vivan los Niños, them kids were badass.

Cuidado con el Ángel.

...was it more connection to your community or personal enjoyment?

Personal enjoyment. I made my dad buy a One Direction backpack
knowing everybody was into Justin Bieber.
I liked stuff that people didn't like but they were still popular-ish.
Like the second option.

...

...do you think your habits have changed over time?

Now it's like finding a balance between what's real and what's not real.
And also with AI, a lot of stuff tends to be fake nowadays.
Continuously learning how to be grounded
and what you believe in and not letting others shape your values.

...

...how kids use the internet now compared to back then?

It's way too excessive now. They need to be stopped.
There's no childhood like back then.
Go color. Go mix. Like how they used to.
You could literally be inside and do so much.
My sister used to make slime. Now she doesn't make anything.

...

...any part of the early online world you miss?

The safety of it. I feel like back then I was safer.
Or maybe a delusion because we were young.
But back then there was not so many things that could overwhelm somebody.
Now with Instagram you have to get it at such a young age.
The pressure of peers.

...

...what role do you think internet and media play in shaping who you are?

Media influences culture and your values and what you perceive as your reality.
What we consume shapes our way of thinking and socializing.
Being more selective about what you're putting your mind and time to is very important.
We shouldn't just be consuming garbage. We have to analyze what we consume.
Algorithms have shaped us. We get a curated algorithm based on what you're looking for.
It's not as diverse. Every app is trying to tailor to get you to buy something
or make you align with what you think.
