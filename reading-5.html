Before the Eno documentary, I hadn't really thought of music as more than just creative - I thought music must originate from just talented people, and I felt that I could never do so. It was encouraging to hear that one of Eno's most commercially successful songs was made almost entirely by an electronic instrument that didn't require a specialization in an instrument that often takes a lot of time and money to master. I also liked how he made a song for a guy (or it was a band?) by playing a game in the studio. It has made me rethink the process of making music and the classical training needed to make music.
